Linear Regression

Linear Regression, April 2, 2014
linear regression is one of the most widely used regression models. The model seek to identify a linear relationships between the dependent and independent variable(s). It models the expected value of a quantitative response (dependent) variable from one or more explanatory variables (independent variables).

Simple Linear Regression and ordinary least squares (OLS)

simple linear regression involves predicting a quantitative response variable from a single explanatory variable Let consider a simple linear regression to determine how the number of flyers sold on a day relate or determine the Sales of the day defined by:

$$Y_{i} = \beta_{0} +\beta_{1}X_{i} + \epsilon _{i}$$

where
  • \(\epsilon _{i}\) are assumed to be independent random variables having a normal distribution with mean zero and constant variance \(\sigma^{2}\)
  • \(Y_{i}\)=Sales for the ith day
  • \(X_{i}\)=number of flyers sold on the ith day
  • \(\beta_{0} \)=the intercept of the line (the predicted amount of sales when no flyers is sold)
  • \(\beta_{1}\)=the slope of the line (the slope represent the change in Sales (Y) for a unit change in the number of flyers (X) sold))
    •  Using Simple Linear Regression, the model assumes there is approximately a linear relationship between the number of flyers (X) sold on a day and the Sales(Y) for that day defined by \( Y \approx \beta_{0} +\beta_{1}X \)

    NOTE

    The intercept and slope are unknown constant of the model known as the model coefficients or parameters.

    In order to predict the expected amount of Sales for a day based or conditioned on the number of flyers sold, the unknown parameters \( \beta_{0}\) and \(\beta_{1}\) are estimated using the training data as \(\hat \beta_{0}\) and \(\hat \beta_{1}\) such that

    $$\hat y_{i}=\hat \beta_{0} +\hat \beta_{1}X_{i}$$

    where $\hat y_{i} $ is the expected predicted Sales (Y) of the day conditioned on the number of flyers (the ith value of X ) sold on that day using the estimated parameters. Using the model our goal is to predict the expected amount of Sales (y) for a day based or conditioned on the number of flyers sold defined as

    $$E(Y)\approx \hat \beta_{0} +\hat \beta_{1}X_{i}$$

    The \(\hat \beta_{0}\) and \(\hat \beta_{1}\) are estimated in such a way that minimize the residual sum of squares (RSS or \(SS_{res}\)) errors defined as

    $$RSS=SS_{res}=e^{2}=\sum_{i}^{n}(y_{i}-\hat y_{i})^{2}$$
